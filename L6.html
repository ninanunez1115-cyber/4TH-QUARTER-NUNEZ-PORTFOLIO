Browser Output and Reflection on Lesson 6

Reflection: In Lesson 6, I learned how CSS can be used to control the size, visibility, and behavior of elements on a webpage. The lesson helped me understand that dimensions such as width, height, and visibility are important in arranging and organizing webpage content properly. I realized that by adjusting these properties, I can make certain parts of a webpage stand out or stay hidden while still keeping the layout balanced. I also learned about pseudo-classes like link, visited, hover, and active, which change how links or other elements react when the user interacts with them. These make webpages more dynamic and interactive. Overall, this lesson taught me that combining dimensions and pseudo-classes can greatly improve both the design and user experience of a website.
