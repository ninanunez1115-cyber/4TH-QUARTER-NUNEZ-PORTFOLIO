Reflection on the Math tiktok competition!



What is the most important thing I learned from the event?

The most important thing I learned from the Math TikTok competition was that learning mathematics does not always have to be done in a traditional way. Sometimes creative approaches can make mathematical concepts easier to understand and more interesting to learn. In our case, we decided to present the concept of an isosceles trapezoid through a short film instead of simply explaining it. This experience showed me that creativity can help make difficult topics more engaging.



How can I apply what I learned in real-life situations?

I can apply this lesson by trying to approach problems from different perspectives. When something seems difficult to understand, finding a creative way to explain or visualize it can make it easier. This skill can be useful not only in mathematics but also in other subjects and real-life problem-solving situations.



Did I actively participate in the event? How?

Yes, I actively participated as the director of our Math TikTok video. I helped plan the scenes, guide the filming process, and make sure that the concept of the isosceles trapezoid was clearly explained in our video. Even though the deadline was very tight, our team managed to film all the scenes in one day and complete the editing the following day.



If I were to teach this topic or subject to a classmate, how would I explain it?

If I were teaching this topic to a classmate, I would explain that an isosceles trapezoid is a trapezoid where the two non-parallel sides are equal in length. Because of this, the angles at the base are also equal. I would also try to use drawings or examples to help make the concept easier to understand.



Why is it important to have events like this?

Events like the Math TikTok competition are important because they encourage students to learn mathematics in creative and engaging ways. Instead of only focusing on solving problems, students are able to explore new ways of explaining and presenting mathematical concepts. This makes learning more enjoyable and memorable.
